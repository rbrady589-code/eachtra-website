Where we began

Three weeks from the doors. A tight budget, and against it a rare production and a clear vision worth carrying. So almost all the real work happened before a single euro was spent — in strategy. Not how to shout louder, but how to make a little travel far: the right partners to carry it the last mile, content sourced and shaped with the tools already in our hands, and spend kept lean enough to last every one of those weeks.

The creative

No budget for a production, so we made one from what we had — content sourced and shaped in house, with every tool we could reach. The piece that travelled furthest wasn't polished at all: a collaboration with the festival's own sound-system partner.

The crossing

With the plan set, the spend moved in three measured steps — never volume for its own sake, always the next right move.

PHASE 01
Ignition
Engagement
Built the warm audience first. Reached 128,000+ people, generated 50,000+ post engagements, and seeded the custom audiences and lookalikes that powered everything after.
PHASE 02
Convert
Traffic
The engine. Tested artist-led, sound-system, and experience angles across Berlin, Amsterdam, Brussels and Vienna — 6,661 real ticket-page visits across the run, the best ad sets clicking at €0.11–0.14.
PHASE 03
Close
Retargeting
Retargeted everyone who showed real intent — visited the ticket page but hadn't purchased — with the strongest creative and urgency in the final stretch. Sales accelerated hardest in the last days.
What the road taught us
Real partnerships beat produced content.
A collab post with the festival's sound-system partner became the single best-performing ad of the campaign.
International audiences converted like locals.
Amsterdam, Brussels and Vienna clicked through within cents of Berlin-only campaigns — festival travel intent is real and targetable.
Efficiency beat the account's own history.
€0.18 blended CPC against a €0.27 historical average — best ad sets hit €0.11–0.14, less than half the norm.
